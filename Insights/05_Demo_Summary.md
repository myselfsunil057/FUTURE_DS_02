# 📚 Customer Churn Analysis Dataset — Executive Summary

---

## 🧠 Overview

This dataset captures **user-level behavioral, technical, and subscription data** designed to analyze customer churn patterns and retention dynamics.

It enables structured evaluation of **user engagement behavior, platform performance impact, subscription effectiveness, and churn risk drivers** across a digital service ecosystem.

The dataset is optimized for **BI dashboards, churn analytics, and predictive retention modeling**.

---

## 🎯 Business Objective

The dataset is built to support data-driven decisions across core retention and product functions:

* Measure overall customer churn and retention health
* Identify key behavioral drivers of user drop-off
* Analyze device, network, and performance impact on churn
* Evaluate subscription plan effectiveness
* Improve retention through predictive and segmented insights

---

## 📊 Key Performance Indicators

* **Total Users:** 4,600
* **Active Users:** 3,188
* **Churned Users:** 1,412
* **Churn Rate:** 30.70%

---

## 📦 Data Structure Summary

The dataset contains **user-level behavioral records** enriched with demographic, technical, subscription, and engagement attributes.

### Core Data Elements:

* User identifiers (user-level tracking)
* Demographic attributes (gender, age group)
* Content preference (top genre)
* Subscription details (plan, previous plan)
* Payment behavior (payment method, auto-renewal status)
* Device attributes (device type)
* Network conditions (network type)
* Performance metrics (buffering time average)
* Target variable (churn status)

---

## 🔗 Business Logic Overview

* Each record represents a **unique user profile snapshot**
* Subscription history enables lifecycle behavior tracking
* Device + network attributes represent **experience quality layer**
* Buffering time acts as a **technical friction indicator**
* Genre preference links engagement behavior to retention patterns
* `is_churned` represents the **final retention outcome**

## ⚠️ Key Business Observations

* Overall churn rate is **high (30.70%)**, indicating retention inefficiency
* Mobile users (Android/iOS) show **higher churn than web users**
* Buffering time strongly correlates with churn probability
* Non-auto-renew users are significantly more likely to churn
* Premium plans show better retention stability than lower tiers
* Core engagement exists in the 18–35 age group, but churn is also concentrated here
* Network quality is a major driver of user experience degradation
